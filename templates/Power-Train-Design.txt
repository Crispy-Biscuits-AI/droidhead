## Quick “fill-and-go” variants for common diagram types

### 1) Wiring / Power Schematic

> Create a **clean electrical schematic** for **[project]**. Include **battery [chemistry, V, Ah]**, **BMS [cells/series rating]**, **PDB [model]**, **DC-DC [in/out, A]**, **Jetson Orin Nano [power spec]**, **fan [voltage/current]**, **fuse [A]**, **switch [spec]**, **connectors [types]**, and **wire gauges per segment**.
> Use **IEC/IEEE symbols**, red=V+, black=GND, blue=signals, green=chassis. Flow **left→right** from source to load. Label test points **TP-1..TP-N** with expected voltages. Include a legend, title block, and safety notes (reverse polarity, ESD). Output **4096×3072 px PNG** + **SVG**, white background, no shadows.

### 2) Block Diagram (System Architecture)

> Generate a **top-level block diagram** of **[system]** showing **Power**, **Compute**, **Sensors/Actuators**, **Comms**, and **Cooling**. Use uniform rounded rectangles, arrows with direction, and port labels. Color groups subtly, provide a legend and title block. **A4 landscape**, **transparent background** variant too.

### 3) Exploded Assembly View

> Produce an **exploded isometric view** of **[assembly]** with stack order, standoffs, screws, and keep-out zones. Callouts show **part name, size, material, qty**. Keep lines crisp (no textures). **3508×2480 px**, white background.

### 4) Panel/Label Layout

> Draw a **front panel layout** with hole diameters, center-to-center spacing, and clearance zones. Annotate with **engraving text** and **iconography** (flat, 1-stroke). Use **mm**, **1:1 scale**, **PDF/SVG**.

## Tips that noticeably improve results

* **Lead with constraints.** Put “Do NOT use gradients/shadows/3D” early.
* **Enforce standards.** Name the symbol standard (IEC/IEEE) and label grammar.
* **Specify flow & grouping.** “Left→right, grouped by power/control” reduces messy layouts.
* **Give a color map.** Models reliably honor simple color→meaning rules.
* **Ask for two outputs.** High-res PNG *and* SVG/PDF for editing.
* **Use numbered callouts + legend.** Increases clarity and consistency.
